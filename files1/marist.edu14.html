Meet Jenny (Hoover) Bonnano '97 

What have you been up to since graduation?

After graduation, I returned home to Delaware and worked at a nonprofit as a marketing assistant and I was the host of a local cable TV show. In 1999, I left the nonprofit agency and started to freelance as a production assistant in the tri-state area, but mostly in Philadelphia. I worked my way up from a P.A. and was getting freelance jobs as an assistant producer and associate producer. I then moved to Philly and worked at a post-production agency. In 2002, I met my husband and the next year, I moved to Connecticut. I worked for a video production company in Connecticut, but then I decided to open my own company called Connect the Dots Productions. I produced, wrote, and directed corporate, government, nonprofit, and small-business videos. In 2010, I took a break from the industry to raise my two kids. I missed working, so I worked part-time at a theatre school and at a local college. As the kids started to get older, I decided to get back into the video industry, and in 2018, I started Jenny B Communications. I was doing pretty well for almost two years. Then the pandemic hit. All my jobs got canceled and colleagues started leaving the industry trying to figure out what to do next. I decided to start exploring other options as well as I was not hopeful that the small industry of video in my area of Connecticut would come back any time soon. I did some reflecting and decided to take my experience in a new direction.

I love to cook. I am a foodie. I am passionate about new flavors and new foods. I love creating recipes. I am concerned about food insecurity, especially when I was witnessing food pantry lines increasing due to the pandemic. I have a communications degree and a lot of video production experience! So, I decided to take my love of food to the next level. In July 2020, I enrolled in a program to become a certified professional cook and in December I completed my certification. I then created my third company, Jenny B Cooks, and I offer live, virtual, small group cooking classes for both adults and kids as well as private parties. Eventually I want to partner with local agencies to teach cooking and cooking techniques to their clients to teach them how to cook healthy and nutritious food for themselves and their families. So that is what I have been up to for the past 24 years!

What is your fondest memory of Marist?

My fondest memories of Marist are the friends I made, especially the ones I have stayed in touch with to this day. Because of our time together at Marist, I have a special connection with those friends and a bond that will last a lifetime. We have gone to each other’s weddings and we see each other and our families whenever possible! 

What is your favorite place on campus and why?

My favorite place was down near the river, which looks so very different now! I remember walking underneath the overpass to get to the river. It was so peaceful and when the weather was nice, it was just a great place to relax. When we drove our cars underneath the overpass, I remember we would beep to make sure the cars on the other side knew people were driving through. Now I see there is a light there!

 
What was your favorite class at Marist? 

My favorite class I believe was called TV News I. We went out into the community and interviewed people for a piece we had to produce. I did alternative medicine and it was so great and fascinating and I learned a lot. It was so cool to interview alternative medicine healers and videotape acupuncture. I also realized that I enjoyed producing, directing, writing, and interviewing once I created that piece. 

How have you remained involved with Marist since graduating?

I attended our 20th reunion which was a lot of fun and it was great to see people I hadn’t seen or stayed in touch with since graduation. I also brought my family for a visit a couple of years ago. While many physical parts of campus have changed, I still remember how it was from 1993 to 1997, and all of those great memories came pouring back. I loved giving a tour of the campus and sharing stories with my husband and two kids.

How did Marist prepare you for your success?

Marist prepared me for success by being flexible and by taking risks. Producing video and journalism pieces pushed me out of my comfort zone and made me realize that interpersonal and communication skills are so important when dealing with the public. My degree has allowed me to use it in many different ways and allowed me to apply it in many different industries.

Think back to your freshman year; what advice would you self-give or offer to another student?

I would tell another student, don’t worry too much. Venture outside of your circle. There were many people who came from Connecticut, New York, and Massachusetts, and many of them already knew each other. I think I was the only one from Delaware in my class. Be open to meeting new people and be open to taking risks in your classes and in your schoolwork.

If you’re not in your office working, where are we most likely to find you?

I am always cooking, even before starting Jenny B Cooks! You will also find me spending time with my family at the beach, hiking, or exploring parks. You will also find me at my daughter’s competitive dance competitions and my son’s baseball games. I also enjoy yoga. And probably like many people, I can’t wait to travel again!
