Elizabethtown College is celebrating Women’s History Month with a series of events throughout the month of March. The Office of Diversity, Inclusion, and Title IX is hosting the series that will recognize and commemorate the vital role of women in American history.

Alumnae Named Teacher of the Year Finalists
Two Elizabethtown College alumnae were among five finalists for the 2021 Harford County (Maryland) Teacher of the Year award. Congratulations to Kerrie Bauer ’98 and Sarah Scholl ’05 on being named finalists! A surprise announcement of the winner will take place during this year’s virtual celebration, scheduled for April 21.

Despite dealing with the challenges of missing a year due to my battle with lymphona, E-town was always there for me. It was my motivation for getting well and getting back to my life. I’ll always appreciate the people—students, faculty, and staff—that taught me so much and helped me get through. The friends that I retain to this day are still such a big part of my life, and I’ll always be thankful for that.
- Jonathon "Jon" Jefferies, Class of 2008
My time since graduating Etown has been spent serving the children of New York City, ringing true “Educate for Service”. Friendships formed at Etown are now lifelong friends who became part of my family.
- Laura La Sala, Class of 1997

As a Blue Jay, I belong to a long tradition of success, not only with what the College has accomplished, but with what I and thousands of other graduates have accomplished.  To continue this legacy, I proudly give back to the college by volunteering in numerous ways. It is my hope that my actions will inspire current students, and other graduates to give back to the college in whatever way they can.

- Mick Pugliese, Class of 1977
Blue Jays are native to North America and are known for their powerful spirit, fierceness, determination, truthfulness, passion and curiosity.  Being an Etown Blue Jay allowed me to develop these traits which now define who I am and how I conduct myself  in my personal and professional life. I appreciate E-town college for providing me with a well-rounded education both academically and socially. Proud to be a Blue Jay!
- Patty Robinson, Class of 1986
