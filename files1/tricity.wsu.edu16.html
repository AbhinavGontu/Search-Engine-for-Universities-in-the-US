WSU no longer using SAT and ACT in admissions process

PULLMAN, Wash. - The Washington State University Board of Regents voted today to stop requiring and using the SAT and ACT tests in the admissions process. The plan approved by regents also recommended that WSU no longer use SAT or ACT scores in the selection process for scholarships as well as tuition waivers. “This is the trend,” Provost and Executive Vice President Elizabeth Chilton said. “I think COVID just sort of pushed a number of colleges and universities a little further along because we saw that we were able to make informed decisions without those scores.”
