Affordability

With an average net price of $25,032 (after grants and scholarships) and a commitment to review competitive awards, Norwich University has a financial aid program that positions us as one of the most affordable private educations in the nation.

Community

At Norwich, you become part of something—a tight-knit community with a strong common identity.

  • “I am constantly reminded of why I chose to come to Norwich. The Corps of Cadets ensures that students are up and efficient in the mornings and develop better time management skills. In the Corps, you gain a whole new family. Rook brothers and sisters not only spend a lot of quality time together, but also support each other even after graduation.”
    Isabella Ferraro '22 International Studies Major
  • "Norwich is one heck of a school and it definitely prepares you for life when you take advantage of what's presented to you. In my time here I have: been on a service trip for a week to Puerto Rico, spent 3 weeks building a dormitory in Africa, had a paid internship, studied away in the Caribbean, and worked at a job that completely changed my career path and helped me realize my true purpose and passion."
    Brieann Wallace '21 Criminal Justice Major
  • "I chose Norwich because I wanted more out of my college experience than just classes and a dorm. Norwich gave me opportunities that you can't get anywhere else: a military lifestyle, a family environment, leadership opportunities, and challenges. That's what I wanted."
    Christopher Cole '15 Chemistry major, Navy ROTC Scholar
  • "Every time I work with a student on a research project or teach a class, I'm sure I learn as much as they do. This is why I teach at Norwich. I never grow tired of that experience and the opportunity to watch our students develop as scientists and people."
    Laurie Grigg, Ph.D. Dept. of Earth & Environmental Science
