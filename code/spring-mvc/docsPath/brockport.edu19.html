We want to hear from you. Update us on your personal and professional life, milestones, achievements, weddings, births, and more in one of two ways:

  1. Submit a class note online.
  2. Email your name, class year, photos, and relevant information to alumni@brockport.edu.

The latest class notes will be posted the first Thursday of every month in conjunction with the release of new editions of The Port. Enjoy recent submissions below.

Class Notes: March 2021

 1960s

Greg Van Dussen '69 wrote a book called "Transfiguration and Hope: A Conversation across Time and Space," published by Wipf & Stock (2018). He published a second book, "Circuit Rider Devotions: Reflections from the Lives of Early Methodist Preachers in North America" (Emeth Press, 2019). Since then, he has written a second volume of "Circuit Rider Devotions" and a fourth book called "Circuit Riders on the Road to Glory," which focuses on early North American preachers' experiences and convictions on death, eternity, and motivation for a life of service. Both are published by Emeth Press.

1970s

Howard Miller '72 is getting ready to sell his business and retire.

 

Anne Gavin Figueroa '75 has been elected as chairperson of the Board of Trustees for Mott Community College in Flint, MI.

 1990s

Troy Decker '94 is married to wonderful Resident Assistant, Kristyn Czerkies Decker, from Briggs Hall! The couple met 30 years ago on campus and have been together ever since. Brockport is a very special place for them.

2000s

 

Velvet Spicer '00 has been named president of Rochester Women's Network, Rochester's premiere personal and professional development organization for women and their allies.

 

Sheila Strong '01/'04 has been named Director of Leadership Development and Equity for United Way of Greater Rochester.

 2010s

 

Sarah Murphy Abbamonte '13 has joined Grassroots Analytics Non-Profit (GANP) as Business Development Manager. GANP, which launched in January 2021, partners with non-profit organizations to optimize and streamline their fundraising efforts.
