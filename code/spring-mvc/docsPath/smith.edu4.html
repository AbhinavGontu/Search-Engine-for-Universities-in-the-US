Pandemic: One Year

elianthvia:

When does the present become history, experience memory?

A year ago, on March 10, 2020, we received an email from President McCartney that marked the beginning of what we all know now as the pandemic world: students were sent home for remote learning, and we had to untangle all the mess during the extended spring break. One day later, WHO declares the coronavirus outbreak a global pandemic.

The email to the community was sent out at 1:18 PM, two minutes before my anthropology class in Seelye started. I was walking briskly on Green Street from Tyler to Seelye, climbing a hill that went around the Neilson Library construction site, running late as usual, after having a spontaneous, joyful lunch conversation with someone on the topic of our favorite childhood cartoon shows (mine is Legend of Nezha that aired from 2003 to 2004).

The moment I walked into the classroom, I immediately sensed some strange air around me. Are people looking at me weirdly because I am late to class again? I thought to myself, half blushed. As I froze at the entrance, a girl rushed out of the classroom holding her phone and started sobbing. I also saw another girl down the hall talking to someone on the phone, her eyes red and watery. Perplexed, I walked into the classroom and settled down in the seat where I usually sat. Then, I whispered to the person sitting next to me: What happened? ‚ÄúHave you checked your email?‚Äù She asked. Still dizzy and out of breath from sprinting to class, I pulled out my phone instinctively and saw the headline of the email notification. My heart sank. We were kicked out.

The rest of the day was a blur. What I learned from the lecture I don‚Äôt remember; I only remember the prof expressing his frustration and the look on students‚Äô faces that showed their minds elsewhere. Having just returned to the states from China, the then-epicenter, I was having a major d√©j√† vu. A day later, I wrote: ‚ÄúI saw it coming. But when the eviction finally hits, I am still overwhelmed. I‚Äôve kind of been preparing for this mentally [after Amherst announced their plan to evict students], but I know I am never mentally ready to deal with this. [Keeping myself distracted is] the only thing that drags me away from the edge of insanity. I need to be busy.‚Äú 

Since the start of Spring 2020, I had been trying to keep myself busy and staying engaged. It was mid-March when I finally got a good sense of my schedule and figured out how everything worked. I knew what assignments I was supposed to turn in each week by heart, where I needed to be for practice without a reminder, when my work shift started, and who I could hang out with on the weekend. Suddenly all aspects of my life came to a pause.

It was only my second semester at Smith. The flowers outside Northrop-Gillet were budding. The New England earth was coming back to life after its long wintry slumber. Spring was so close, but I had to wave goodbye to it all.

First Two Weeks: Food, Classes, and Trevor Noah

Made it through the first two weeks of Spring 2021 and the initial operating mode! Hello March and goodbye Blue Mode! 

The start of the semester was like a whirlwind. The strangeness, excitement and novelty of this hybrid semester took me a bit to get used to, especially since I am living on campus with covid restrictions.

Different Food, Same Food 

Today (Feb 28, almost March!) is the last day of the Blue Mode, so food is still for take-out only. In a normal year, students go to the dining hall of their choice to have meals. As of now, however, we order food using Grubhub. The app gives us several dining hall options that each have their own menu. We can customize our meal order on Grubhub, and the app will notify us once the food is ready. We then pick up our food packed in paper bags and eat in our rooms. Just like a real restaurant, the dining halls only open during certain hours. Because the menu rarely changes, some students invented monikers for each dining hall in accordance with the cuisine. Here are some examples just for fun: Cutter Ziskind - McDonald, Tyler - Pizza Hut, King Scales - Taco Bell, Lamont - Chiptole, Comstock - Panda Express, Chuckett - Panera. (Note: Dawes Gluten-Free Dining and Kosher Kitchen are not included in the list above, but they are open too.)

Positively, thanks to the low positivity rate on campus, some academic buildings have been open to OneCard holders already. So in the past few days instead of eating in my room, I‚Äôve been going to Seelye by myself to eat in an empty classroom. I also noticed that the staff have been setting up tables in the dining hall for social distanced (can we call it physical distanced instead?) seating in preparation for Green Mode which starts on March 1st.

Spring 2012 Classes

While I eat in the classrooms, I study and attend classes in my room (which is kind of ‚Ä¶ weird??). This semester I am taking a total of 21 credits, which include three classes to fulfill the requirements for the engineering major: PHY 210: Math Methods for Physicists and Engineers, EGR 220: Circuit Theory and CHM 222: Organic Chemistry. On why I chose to take the notoriously hard chemistry class as my lab science, the decision making process of which took a good couple of weeks, one: I like challenging classes, two: I want to explore the field of chemistry and the possibility of being a chemical engineer, and three: a lot of my unbiased chemistry major/ minor friends recommended it and told me they had fun; I believed them. 

For circuits and orgo, I received a lab kit and a model kit (a semi-fair compensation for online labs?). Both classes are demanding and the online labs are sometimes confusing and frustrating. I turn to find solace in drawing the hexagons in organic molecules and rectangles in circuits. 

Classes I am taking just for fun:  CLS 227: Classical Mythology, IDP 132: Designing Your Path, and MUS 924Y: Second Year Performance. 

Pictured above is the breadboard we use for EGR 220 lab. It is affectionately nicknamed Adam (short for ADALM 1000 Evaluation Board). Pictured below is the molecular kit for CHM 222. Students use the kit to help them visualize 3D molecular structures.

An Evening with Trevor Noah

Last Tuesday, all students were invited to a live virtual event: An Evening with Trevor Noah: A Moderated Q&A with Heidi Gardner. I am just throwing out some highlights here. Blogger‚Äôs favorite line: If you‚Äôre crazy alone, you‚Äôre just crazy; if you‚Äôre crazy with a bunch of other people, you‚Äôre having fun. Trevor‚Äôs definition of growing up: A mini drunk person getting sober. The Q&A session was a nice, short study break on a busy weeknight.

Virtual events are better than no events, but nothing beats in-person activities. I really look forward to finally moving to Green Mode!

What are the bathrooms in the houses like?

Hi prospective student! Most students will have shared hallway bathrooms with shower stalls and toilet stalls. There is usually a counter of shared sinks too and trash cans, but you will need to bring your own hand towel and sometimes your own soap! There is also usually a single stall bathroom in each house or even on each floor for people who need more space or prefer privacy. Triple rooms usually have their own bathroom that you would be responsible for cleaning yourself. That being said, everyone does their part to keep the shared bathrooms nice and clean.

Spend the day with me: first day edition!

nylainnoho:

Hello Friends, 

Here in Noho, us Smithies are just finishing up our first week of classes! In honor of finishing this first week I wanted to share a day in my life post for the first day of classes. Before we start, I also wanted to show you all my desk setup since this is where all of the magic happens. 

MORNING

8:00 am: Good morning! I am on the track team so my day started with a lift from 8:00 am to 8:45 am, this is something that I will be doing every Monday and Friday for the entire semester. It usually struggle with getting up, but since it was the first day, I had the first day jitters and didn‚Äôt have much difficulty getting up. 

9:00 am: After I finished lifting, I headed up the road to Tyler. Right now we are ordering all of our meals through GrubHub and you are able to grab and go once your meal is ready. I think I had to wait about 15 minutes for my breakfast to be done that day. 

9:15 am: There are a few public spaces that we are allowed to eat in, but I usually eat in my room and listen to a podcast or talk to friends and family. One of my friends put me on to NPR‚Äôs Life Kit podcast and now I am putting you all on! After my breakfast I also like to spend some time writing in my journal really about anything that comes to my mind. I try to do this frequently (not every single morning), but on the mornings that I feel like it. 

9:35 am: After breaking my fast I took a nice shower and really took my time to get ready. Some mornings I rush, but I wanted to feel extra good for the first day.

10:55 am: Finally, the golden time! This is when my first class, PSY269: Categorization and Intergroup Behavior started! This is a psychology course and some of the main things we will be discussing will be prejudice, discrimination and other things related to how groups interact with each other. I really enjoyed our first class. 

12:00 pm: Class finished a bit early this day, so after I went and grabbed lunch with my friends. I was able to snap a picture of my lunch and this meal is the Indian Curry Bowl from Lamont. I think they are pretty good and probably get this meal for lunch or dinner at least once a week. I usually like to have something to look at or listen to while I eat and on this day I watched Youtube and Facetimed a friend.

AFTERNOON

1:40 pm: After lunch, I started my next class, AFR117: History of African American People to 1960. I also loved this class. We reviewed the syllabus and did a small breakout room activity where we looked at a few quotes and discussed what we thought about them. I think the quote my group looked at was really important so I will share it here. Let me know if you have any thoughts about it or questions about what I thought. 3:15 pm: I had my next class, PSY 202: Research Methods for Psychology. I have taken a class with this professor in the past and I really enjoyed it. Research Methods is also required for the psychology major, so that is part of the reason I am taking this class too.

4:30 pm: Once my Research Methods class ended I had a bit of time until dinner! I will use this time to let you all know that most classes (except labs and maybe a few others) last for an hour and 15 minutes. 

EVENING

5:00 pm: There is a big dinner rush at five and I contribute to it. The most popular dining halls for dinner right now are Tyler and Comstock. This night I had pizza for dinner! 

7:05 pm: After dinner, I had my final class AFR289: Race, Feminism, and Resistance in Movements for Social Change. The professor for this class is amazing and I was so excited to take another one of their classes! This class‚Äìand pretty much all of my classes are discussion-based. 8:20 pm: Finally, at 8:20 pm I was finished with all of my classes. I *try* to stop doing school work at 8:30 pm (although it does not always happen), so I was done for the night. I listened to some music, brushed my teeth and did my other night activities and was off to bed! That was my day!

Thank you, my loves for sticking with me in this post! I am so looking forward to the rest of this semester and I hope that you all are excited too. I am sending you my good energies. Chat soon. Tootles.

‚Äìxo, Nyla in Noho
