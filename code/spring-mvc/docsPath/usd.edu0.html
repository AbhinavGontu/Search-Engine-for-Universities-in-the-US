    March 16, 2021

    South Dakota Special Education Conference 2021

    The annual Special Education Conference provides a unique experience to gather with South Dakota educators, related service providers, and parents. This virtual event allows participants to learn about evidence-based innovations, educational trends leading towards best practices, and provide practical solutions for special education classrooms. For more information, go to https://www.usd.edu/medicine/center-for-disabilities/sd-special-education-conference

    March 16, 2021

    Entering Mentoring

    The USD-HHMI Inclusive Science Initiative and the Center for Teaching and Learning invite you to participate in the Entering Mentoring Workshop, a facilitator-guided workshop that teaches the principles and best practices of mentoring undergraduate and graduate students. The curriculum has been developed and validated by the nationally recognized Center for the Improvement of Mentored Experiences in Research or CIMER (https://cimerproject.org/). The workshop is open to faculty, staff, and graduate students. Participants must attend 8 of the 10 sessions to receive the Statement of Achievement in Mentoring. Please email ctl@usd.edu to register.

    March 16, 2021

    LETTERPRESS: From Industry to Art

    University Art Galleries, in collaboration with Assistant Professor of Graphic Design, James Quigley, co-curated LETTERPRESS: From Industry to Art. This educational exhibition features works from the University of South Dakota's permanent collection and Contemporary Letterpress artists from across the United States.

    USD's First Artificial Intelligence Symposium

    USD's first Artificial Intelligence Symposium aims to unite academia, industry and government AI & Data Engineering experts to solve current challenges in various applications such as healthcare, cyberthreats, quantum computing, sustainable agriculture and risk management. Registration is FREE for this Zoom event. Register at www.usd.edu/arts-and-sciences/ai/symposium. The Zoom link needed to participate in the Symposium will be provided as soon as possible.

    Spatial Projects with StoryMap JS

    In the fourth installment of the Digital Humanities workshop series, learn how to compose spatial digital stories in a user-friendly interface. With StoryMapJS, your students can chart events on locations from the OpenStreetMap project, imaginary maps, and even images and art pieces. This workshop will be offered as a synchronous Zoom session. Please email ctl@usd.edu to register.

    Spring Leadership Seminar Series: Group Dynamics & Creating an Inclusive Environment

    Leadership is all about being able to inspire a group of people towards a common goal. One aspect of that is the creation of an inclusive environment where participants can express themselves holistically and authentically. In this session, you will learn the tools to navigating group dynamics (including conflict) and how to create an inviting space for everyone.

    March 18, 2021

    Women's History Month Lecture by Martha S. Jones "Vanguard: Leading on Voting Rights, Leading the Nation"

    A joint Schell History Lecture and Gunderson Lecture in Honor of Women's History Month on Zoom. Professor Martha S. Jones is the Society of Black Alumni Presidential Professor and Professor of History at The Johns Hopkins University. She is a legal and cultural historian whose work examines how Black Americans have shaped the story of American democracy. Sponsored by the Department of History with support from the Knudson School of Law, Office for Diversity, Center for Diversity & Community, University of South Dakota Libraries, and Women, Gender & Sexuality Studies Program. To register for the event, visit https://www.usd.edu/arts-and-sciences/history/vanguard-lecture
