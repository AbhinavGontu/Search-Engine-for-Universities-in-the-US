Campus Chat: A series of fun Friday lunch talks

 The Office of Alumni Relations invites you to join some of your favorite faces on Fridays during the lunch hour.  Reconnect with professors and staff you remember from college days or get an introduction to someone you would like to know. 

We will start at 12:30. The first half will be a conversation with our special guest and the second half will be open for alumni to engage with the guest and each other. We will wrap up before 1 p.m. Bring your lunch and take a break with your Randolph-Macon friends, old and new.  We welcome your suggestions for future special guests by emailing
Alice Lynch at AliceLynch@rmc.edu.

Join us at this link: https://rmc.zoom.us/j/2592540111
Email Diane Munsey at
DianeMunsey@rmc.edu for the calendar reminder

List of Upcoming Chats: 
March 5th - Ed Showalter '84, Professor of Economics and Business
March 12th - John McManus '84, Assoc. Professor of Computer Science
March 19th - Adam Pulver, Director of Luxe Show Choir
March 26th - Alicia Elms, Director of Diversity & Multicultural Affairs

 

Hidden In Plain Site: Richmond
March 25, 2021 at 12 noon

 

Join Dontrese Brown, Executive Director of the EDGE, and Dean Browell '98 for a discussion about their incredible new project exploring the Black History of Richmond, Virginia. Through a VR exploration of distinct, but easy to overlook sites around Richmond, VA, they will help to tell the story of the Black experience throughout history. Featuring actual examples from various angles and ages, these sites will be brought to impactful life through current appearance augmented with historical imagery. Our goal is to inform and educate while changing how residents and visitors see and experience their city.

Register for our discussion here.

Check out their website at HiddenInPlainSite.org

Donate to the R-MC Annual Fund

The COVID-19 pandemic has impacted our students, faculty, staff, and the entire campus community. Randolph-Macon College safely and successfully brought students back on campus this past fall, and we are preparing to do the same for the Spring 2021 semester. There are still many needs and costs associated with planning. The College provided each person with a personal protective equipment kit, while classroom, learning, and other campus spaces were modified to help protect the health of “The Hive.” Your continued support will make a significant impact on the lives of our students and their futures!

Please consider a gift to The Randolph-Macon Annual Fund which provides the College with the fiscal flexibility to innovate and respond quickly and decisively to our students’ greatest needs, especially in these challenging times.

Gifts can be made online at www.rmc.edu/givenow or by calling 866-694-4574. We now accept gifts through Venmo @JacketsGiveBack.  Please include your name, class year, and where you want your support directed.

Thank you!
