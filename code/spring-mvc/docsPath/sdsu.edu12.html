Aztec Culture



Did you know?


The Aztecs' higher education system was divided into areas of expertise. For example the Calmecac education focused on history, art, philosophy and governance, and the Cuicacalli education focused on military expertise.

The Aztecs had compulsory education for all children and those who did well could go on to higher education.

The Aztecs were a highly literate society that maintained extensive records on their history, religious practices, accounting and governance, as well as beautiful poetry and philosophical narratives.

The temples of the Aztecs were built with stones that weighed in excess of 40 tons, demonstrating advanced skills in engineering.

The three pillars of the Aztec Empire are Knowledge, Strength and Prowess, and Giving Back to the Community.

The Aztecs used Xochitl, the word for flower, in many aspects of their poetic language. In their rituals and celebrations, music and song came together to express beauty, love and a desire to reach out to other worlds.

In Aztec culture the combination of red and black signified literacy and education.

There are two types of Aztec calendars:

  • One, called the xiuhpohualli, has 365 days. It describes the days and rituals related to the seasons, the agricultural year or the solar year.
  • The other, called the tonalpohualli, has 260 days. It is a term meaning "the count of days."
