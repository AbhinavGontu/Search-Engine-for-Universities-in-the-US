Consortium Enabling Cybersecurity Opportunities & Research (CECOR) of the University of the Virgin Islands

  • UVI is one of seventeen partners in the K-20 CECOR Project

  • The CECOR Project will develop a cybersecurity workforce

  • Cybersecurity is now one of the US's major priorities

  • UVI's CECOR program offers workshops,forums, and camps

Building Cybersecurity Pipeline in the U.S. Virgin Islands and Beyond

In support of the National Initiative for Cybersecurity Education (NICE) program,  the University of the Virgin Islands  in partnership with CECOR (Consortium Enabling Cybersecurity Opportunities & Research)  will recruit  students at all level and engage them in cybersecurity activities, offer scholarships for talented youths, provide summer  training opportunities for undergraduates  and teachers  and will create robust cybersecurity education and training programs.

Announcements: UVI's CECOR program provides summer programs for students and workshops and forums for professionals on the St. Thomas campus and Albert A. Sheen campus on St.Croix.

SANS – the global leader in cybersecurity training and certification – is hosting our next SANS+HBCU Cyber Ranges competition - Black History Month Edition! This is a FOUR DAY event from Friday, Feb 19, 2021 through Monday Feb 22, 2021. The range will be open for competition on February 19th at 9 am ET and will remain open, around the clock, until it officially closes on February 22nd at 6 pm ET. Participants should feel free to come and go as they please. The Cyber Range will be open around the clock for 81 hours to provide maximum flexibility.

Throughout this four-day event, participants will gain cybersecurity skills by competing in a self-paced, independent, hands-on challenge. The skills gained from our CTF are applicable to real-world jobs and include concepts such as:

  • Using a command line interface
  • Scripting and programming
  • Thinking creatively
  • Interaction with pcaps and flow logs, parsing, extracting, and visualizing packets
  • Introduction and use of common forensic tools
  • Interaction with common developer pitfalls

The University of the Virgin Islands is a learner-centered institution dedicated to the success of its students and committed to enhancing the lives of the people of the U.S. Virgin Islands and the wider Caribbean through excellent teaching, innovative research, and responsive community service.

The University of the Virgin Islands CECOR program's main objective is to increase the number of talented Virgin Islanders in cyber security and the STEM pipelines by engaging K-16 youth and the community in exciting educational, training and undergraduate research activities.
