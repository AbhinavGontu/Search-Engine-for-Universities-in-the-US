MCIC 2021

March 1–31, 2021 • Virtual Series

 

While 2020 may have kept us physically apart, social justice movements demonstrated how our collective voices can make a difference in communities throughout the world. The University of Wisconsin-Platteville is committed to fostering these conversations and inspiring action, while also ensuring a safe environment for all involved. It is this commitment that sparked our new Midwest Culturally Inclusive Conference Series.

Based on the premise of our previous Midwest Culturally Inclusive Conference Series, this new virtual series will provide faculty, staff, students, and the community the opportunity to engage with free trainings, workshops, and events for personal, professional, and academic growth revolving around the topics of diversity, equity, and inclusion. This format will offer participants different opportunities to learn within their professional and academic schedules throughout the month of March.

The Midwest Culturally Inclusive Conference (MCIC) has traditionally been a multi-day, in-person conference that has provided our region with professional and educational opportunities around the topics of diversity, equity and inclusion. In consideration of the ongoing global pandemic and the safety of our participants, MCIC will look different this year. Throughout the month of March 2021, the MCIC Series will be a modified version of what has been offered in the past. This series will provide staff, faculty, students, and the community the ability to engage with free virtual opportunities for personal, professional, and academic growth.

From March 1st to March 31st, we will offer sessions via Zoom. We hope this adaptation of MCIC will offer participants increased accessibility and variety while being mindful of work, academic, and personal schedules. Sessions will cover topics such as: race, LGBTQ+ identity, disability, mental health, intersectionality, and more.

We will be using the event platform, Whova, to host MCIC. This platform is available on mobile devices and through any web browser. Whova features can also be integrated with Google and Outlook calendars, allowing participants to build their own conference schedules. Users can enjoy some of the same benefits of in-person conferences such as community networking, discussion boards, reading session descriptions, and more.

This year’s MCIC Series will prioritize registration to the UW-Platteville institution. All UW-Platteville users will have the opportunity to register, view, and participate in live session offerings. Recordings of these sessions will be provided on our website for public access outside of our institution at any time following the conclusion of the series. Registration and access to the recordings will be free.

Registration opens for our institution in late January. You must register to access the sessions offered.

Please contact Campus Climate at campusclimate@uwplatt.edu or 608.342-.6179 with any questions or concerns!

Keynote Speakers

 

CeCe McDonald 

FREE CeCe: My Journey to Womanhood

March 1 | 6–7 p.m.

In this presentation, Cece McDonald uses storytelling to articulate the personal and political implications of being both black and trans, while fostering important conversations around mass incarceration, sexuality, and violence. With energy and conviction, she highlights the hope she now fights for – that all LGBTQ people can live their lives free of hate and prejudice and confidently pursue their dreams without fear.

Bio: CeCe McDonald is a transgender activist and revered icon of the LGBTQ community. She captured international recognition in 2011 after surviving a white supremacist and transphobic attack, later receiving a second-degree manslaughter conviction and serving 19 months in prison simply for defending herself.

Rolling Stone has praised her as “an LGBT folk hero for her story of survival – and for the price she paid for fighting back.” In 2014, The Advocate included her among its annual "40 Under 40” list of “young LGBT people fighting intersectional battles in our greater community.” Since her release, she has graced stages across the country where she uses storytelling to articulate the personal and political implications of being both black and trans.

 

Haben Girma

Climate Change Solutions Must Include Disabled People

March 30 | 5–6 p.m.

Disabled people are one of the largest underrepresented groups, numbering over 1.3 billion worldwide. Organizations that choose to invest in disability inclusion gain access to a larger audience, provide a better experience for both disabled and nondisabled members, and facilitate growth and innovation. Join us for an engaging presentation on Haben Girma’s journey as a disability rights advocate and how we can make disability inclusion part of climate change solutions. 

Bio: The first Deafblind person to graduate from Harvard Law School, Haben Girma is a human rights lawyer advancing disability justice. President Obama named her a White House Champion of Change. She received the Helen Keller Achievement Award, a spot on the Forbes 30 Under 30 list, and TIME100 Talks. President Bill Clinton, Prime Minister Justin Trudeau, and Chancellor Angela Merkel have all honored Girma. Girma believes disability is an opportunity for innovation, and she teaches organizations the importance of choosing inclusion. The New York Times, Oprah Magazine, and TODAY Show featured her memoir, Haben: The Deafblind Woman Who Conquered Harvard Law.

Series FAQ

Troubleshooting

Are you having trouble finding the Zoom links for the MCIC Series Sessions you want to attend?  Here are some possible solutions:

  1. You are trying to log in using a different email address than the one you registered with. Using a different email address will prompt an invitation code.
  2. You are trying to view the session through the www.uwplatt.edu/mcic website.  Zoom links are not available through this site.  You must access the Zoom links through Whova, either in a web browser or in the app.
  3. You haven’t registered for the MCIC Series. All participants must register for the MCIC Series at www.uwplatt.edu/mcic. This will provide you free access to the conference platform, Whova.  Using Whova in a web browser or in an app will allow you to access all MCIC Series sessions, network, ask questions, and more.
  4. If none of these options work and you are still being prompted with an invitation code, please use: MCICSERIES2021. 

As a reminder, recordings for most sessions will be available on the Campus Climate YouTube page. Please allow for up to three business days for the recording to be posted. Please email the Department of Campus Climate at campusclimate@uwplatt.edu with any questions or concerns.

Who can attend the Midwest Culturally Inclusive Conference Series?

Registration for the MCIC Series is open and free for all UW-Platteville students, staff, and faculty. 

The public will have access to all recorded sessions as soon as possible, after they take place. Recorded sessions can be accessed for free at Midwest Culturally Inclusive Conference Series website.

Will the MCIC Series be in-person?

The MCIC Series will take place virtually via the platform Zoom. Zoom sessions that are a part of the MCIC Series will take place between March 1 through March 31.

I am not a part of the University of Wisconsin-Platteville institution. How can I participate in the MCIC Series?

Those outside of UW-Platteville may view all recorded sessions offered during the MCIC Series on our event website: uwplatt.edu/mcic. These are free and accessible to the public after the session occurs live.

When is the MCIC Series?

The MMIC Series will take place from March 1 through March 31. Throughout the month, panels, presentations, guided dialogues, and more will be offered for participants to engage with. 

We have structured these sessions throughout four weeks because of the feedback we received regarding the inconvenience and struggles with attending an hour-to-hour virtual conference. Participants have shared that they feel “Zoomed Out” or lose focus after an hour or two of virtual engagements. We hope this staggered approach provides increased ease and accessibility of the MCIC Series’ offerings.

Who can I reach out to if I have further questions?

The MCIC Series is primarily lead by the Department of Campus Climate staff. You may contact them at any time at campusclimate@uwplatt.edu or 608.342.6179.

What is the cost of the MCIC Series?

In an effort to make the series as accessible as possible, all sessions will be free this year. Recorded sessions will be posted to our event website (uwplatt.edu/mcic) and will also be available to view for free.

Where can I register for the MCIC Series?

You can find registration information on our event website: uwplatt.edu/mcic. Registration will be provided through the app and website, Whova. Registration is required to attend any session provided through the MCIC Series. 

Do I have to register more than once if I want to attend more than one session?

No; you only have to register once through the Whova app or website. Registering for the entire MCIC Series gets you access to all of the sessions provided through the month of March. 

What is Whova?

Whova is a virtual conference platform that integrates scheduling, session descriptions, registration, networking, message boards, and more all into one. You may access Whova via an internet browser at whova.com or download the free app from the App Store or Google Play Store.

Clicking on the Live Stream links below will not allow you to access the MCIC Series.  You must register for the conference and use Whova to access the Zoom links for all sessions. Please contact Campus Climate (campusclimate@uwplatt.edu) with any questions.

How do you plan to prevent unwanted guests from joining sessions during the MCIC Series?

We will be working closely with all presenters to ensure they understand the various safety features in Zoom. Additionally, a core team of MCIC Committee representatives will be scheduling sessions for presenters as much as possible. Each session will have the same safety features activated to ensure that participants are able to adequately engage and the moderators have control of the virtual space.

We are also working to create participation guidelines for all who engage in the MCIC Series’ events. We hope these serve as parameters for engaging respectfully with one another and the presenters.

Additionally, all presenters will have at least one co-host assigned to their session in an effort to help with transitions, tech concerns, and mitigate any potential harm.

Finally, all participants of the MCIC Series are required to register via the conference platform, Whova. Only those within the UW-Platteville institution will be able to register for the live viewing of sessions offered during this MCIC Series. This is to increase accessibility of the conference for those within our institution as virtual space is limited. Additionally, this will provide us a greater ability to mitigate, track, and follow-up on any potential harmful acts that occur throughout the series.

How long will the recorded sessions stay on your event website?

The recorded MCIC Series’ sessions will be available on our website for 90 days after the live presentation. We will do our best to have the recordings permanently archived and will announce when/if that is available.
