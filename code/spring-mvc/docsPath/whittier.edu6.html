FRANKLIN COUNTY, Ohio —

A dog that was rescued by an Ohio animal shelter is now serving as a K-9 officer.

The Franklin County Sheriff's Office's therapy K-9 unit tweeted on Monday about its newest officer, Tattoo.

Advertisement

The therapy K-9 unit helps select and pair dogs with surrounding police agencies.

The office said their team recently adopted the rescue from the Franklin County Dog Shelter & Adoption Center.

Tattoo will serve as a K-9 officer with the Swatara Township Police Department near Harrisburg, Pennsylvania.
