    Events

    Please note: To protect the health of our community during the COVID-19 pandemic, Wellesley’s campus remains closed to the public for the spring. Events listed here that are being held virtually are open to the public unless otherwise specified. Please check back for updates and visit our Keeping Wellesley Healthy website to learn more about the precautions the College is taking.

    All Events

    • Mar 13, 1 PM, Apr 14, 2:30 PM, May 22, 1 PM
      Wellesley College Guild of Carillonneurs

      The Wellesley College Guild of Carillonneurs carries on the tradition of bell-ringing in Galen Stone Tower, which began when the carillon was first installed in 1931.

      Event Date: 
      Saturday, March 13, 2021 - 1:00pm

      ,

      Event Date: 
      Wednesday, April 14, 2021 - 2:30pm

      ,

      Event Date: 
      Saturday, May 22, 2021 - 1:00pm

    • Mar 17, 2:30 PM
      French House Virtual Lecture Series: Heidi Holst-Knudsen

      Join Wellesley’s French House in welcoming Heidi Holst-Knudsen for her lecture Can French be Inclusive? Considerations of a Contentious Linguistic Project.

      Event Date: 
      Wednesday, March 17, 2021 - 2:30pm

    • Mar 24, 3:30 PM
      Crisis and Opportunity: Building a Better Child Care System

      WCW panelists discuss what the Biden-Harris administration has done so far to support the child care industry, and what still needs to be done to build a sustainable child care system in the U.S., thereby advancing gender equality.

      Event Date: 
      Wednesday, March 24, 2021 - 3:30pm

    • Mar 26, 10–11 AM
      Russia and the U.S., From Trump to Biden

      Why was Donald Trump so supportive of Vladimir Putin? What should the Biden administration’s Russia policy look like? Dr. Angela Stent, former National Intelligence Officer for Russia and Eurasia, will address these key questions.

      Event Date: 
      Friday, March 26, 2021 -
      10:00am to 11:00am

    • Mar 30, 7:30 PM
      Collegium Musicum

      A concert by students playing music from Renaissance courts on instruments from Wellesley's unique collection of early instruments.

      Event Date: 
      Tuesday, March 30, 2021 - 7:30pm

    • Mar 31, 10:30 AM–12:15 PM
      The 2021 Douglas Lecture: Dr. Dorceta Taylor

      The Frost Center for the Environment invites the Wellesley community to hear Dr. Dorceta Taylor deliver the 2021 Douglas Lecture: Wage Inequalities and Funding Disparities in Environmental Organizations.

      Event Date: 
      Wednesday, March 31, 2021 -
      10:30am to 12:15pm

    • Apr 7, 3:30 PM
      2021 Calderwood Lecture in Economics: Melissa Dell

      The Wellesley community is invited to the 2021 Calderwood Lecture in economics; Harvard’s Mellissa Dell will discuss her paper Nation Building Through Foreign Intervention: Evidence From Discontinuities in Military Strategies.

      Event Date: 
      Wednesday, April 7, 2021 - 3:30pm

    • Apr 14, 2:30 PM
      French House Virtual Lecture Series: Jennifer McWeeny

      Join Wellesley’s French House in welcoming Jennifer McWeeny for her lecture Why Beauvoir’s “The Second Sex” Is Not about Gender: Phenomenology, Sexual Difference, and the (Mis)translation of Se faire femme.

      Event Date: 
      Wednesday, April 14, 2021 - 2:30pm

    • Apr 14, 6 PM
      The Betsy Wood Knapp ’64 Lecture

      Join author Beverly Daniel Tatum and Megan Núñez, dean of faculty affairs, for a candid conversation about racial relations in this country, and learn ways to facilitate necessary conversations about race and inclusion among young people.

      Event Date: 
      Wednesday, April 14, 2021 - 6:00pm

    • Apr 16, 7:30 PM
      Guest Artist Public Muse: Del Sol Quartet

      This quartet features music from composers today— ranging from the beautiful, to the joyous, to the political—as part of their spring residency with the music department.

      Event Date: 
      Friday, April 16, 2021 - 7:30pm
