MENOMONIE (October 23, 2017) - The UW-Stout Athletic Hall of Fame displays will have a new look when seven Blue Devil greats are inducted into the Hall of Fame in the spring of 2018.

A new interactive video display replaces the former Hall of Fame showcases in the Sports and Fitness Center next to the entrance to Johnson Fieldhouse. The Wall of Champions will display the names of all of the members in the UW-Stout Athletic Hall of Fame, plus a video station which will allow viewers access to information on the Hall of Fame members, scholar-athletes and all UW-Stout student-athletes who have achieved All-America honors.

Entering the Hall of Fame on April 21, 2018, will be softball player Suzanne (Brion) Crotteau, pole vaulter Jamie Buchholtz, basketball player Kelsey (Duoss) Steinhagen, long distance runner Paul Gilles, baseball player and coach Seth Maier, soccer player Nellie (Sivertsen) Oothoudt and athletic director and coach Steve Terry.

The induction ceremony will be held in the Memorial Student Center on the evening of April 21, 2018. A reception will begin at 5 p.m., with dinner at 6 p.m. The induction ceremony will begin approximately 6:45 p.m.

Tickets can be purchased by filling out the linked forms and sending them back by Friday, April 6.  Additional information can be obtained by calling the UW-Stout athletic office, 715-232-2224. Tickets are $35 and $20 for 12-years-and-under.  Ticker Order Form [PDF]

Sponsorships for the UW-Stout Athletic Hall of Fame are also available. Download a brochure.

The purpose of the UW-Stout Athletic Hall of Fame, which was established in 1978, is to pay tribute and to give deserved recognition to former athletic letter winners, coaches/athletic personnel who have demonstrated outstanding contributions while on campus or since graduation and thereby to enhance school tradition. To nominate a former Blue Devil athlete for consideration into the UW-Stout Athletic Hall of Fame, use the Hall of Fame nomination form. Ten years must have passed since an athlete last competed at Stout to be eligible for consideration.
