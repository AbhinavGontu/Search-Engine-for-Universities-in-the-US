STEP 2: COMPLETE THE ONLINE PLACEMENT ASSESSMENTS

All new and transferring full-time and part-time students who have not accumulated 30 quarter credits are required to take a Placement Assessment test that assesses skills in reading, English, and mathematics, prior to pre-registration. We are currently providing these tests online after the Placement Intake Form has been completed.

Placement Assessments must be taken before registration for classes can occur.

We need to ask a few questions to create a profile for you as a student.  When your placement results are complete they will be placed with your profile and sent on to your advisor along with course recommendations.

STEP 3: SET UP YOUR DIGITAL ACCOUNTS

If you are a new SKC student, we now have an online form that you complete to request your digital accounts. This includes accounts for your SKC Student Email, our ECampus Portal (JICS), our Learning Management System (Schoology), and our Managed Print System (Papercut).

Complete the online form below. Once you submit your form, the SKC Help Desk will create your accounts and email the information to the personal email account that you entered on the form.

DIGITAL ACCOUNT REQUEST FORM

Please include an appropriate photo for your student ID and a photo of a legal ID. A legal ID could be a Tribal Enrollment card, a State ID or Driver's licenses, or a passport.

DON'T HAVE FINANCIAL AID YET?

Submit a FAFSA Application online at studentaid.gov to determine your eligibility for financial aid in the forms of grants, loans, work-study, and scholarships. Add our SKC school code: 015023. After you submit the FAFSA, SKC Financial Aid will send an email notification requesting you to complete your financial aid file.

Contact the Financial Aid Office for assistance at 406-275-4857.

Step 5: Online New Student Orientation

Before starting the next quarter we will be hosting a self-paced Online NSO. Spring Quarter NSO 2020-21 will be open beginning February 8, 2021 and closing March 28, 2021.

After completing the Online NSO, you will communicate with an Academic Advisor to review your degree plan and finalize your class schedule. After your Advising session and completing a placement assessment your Advisor will assist you in registering for classes.
