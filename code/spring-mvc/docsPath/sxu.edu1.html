SXU ATHLETE OF THE MONTH
SENIOR AARON KUMMER
MEN'S VOLLEYBALL

Honorable Mentions

  • Nadia Atton (Women's Volleyball)
  • Claire Austin (Women's Basketball)
  • Daniel Barrios (Baseball)
  • Mike Iturbe (Football)
  • Conor Michaels (Men’s Track & Field)
  • Josh Niego (Men’s Basketball)

ATHLETES OF THE MONTH

Senior Aaron Kummer (Men's Volleyball)

  • Named the NAIA Setter of the Week on February 9 and again on March 2 and became the first player for men’s volleyball to earn National Player of the Week honors more than once in a season.
  • Was named CCAC Setter of the Week also on February 9 and March 2.
  • Handed out 395 assists, averaging 10.6 assists per set in 10 matches.
  • Set the single-match record for assists in three sets with 46 at Judson (Ill.) on February 22.

Senior Nadia Atton (Women's Volleyball)

  • Led the Cougars with 86 kills and was tied for the team lead with 11 service aces.
  • Posted a team-best 63 digs and was third in blocks with 13.
  • Scored a team-high 103.5 points in six matches for SXU.

Junior Claire Austin (Women's Basketball)

  • Averaged a double-double with 16.3 points per game and 11.5 rebounds per game.
  • Shot a team-best 58.1 percent (36-for-62) from the field, including 50.0 percent on 10 attempts.
  • Blocked a team-leading 15 shots and tied for the team lead with 16 steals.

Freshman Daniel Barrios (Baseball)

  • Hit .333 (11-for-33), with 15 runs scored, 14 RBI’s, four doubles and three home runs.
  • Was a perfect 5-for-5 in stolen base attempts, drew four walks and slugged .727.
  • Hit his first career Grand Slam against William Penn (Iowa) on February 23.

Junior Mike Iturbe (Football)

  • Named NAIA Special Teams Player of the Week for the third time in his career on March 1.
  • Also named Mid-States Football Association (MSFA) Midwest League Special Teams Player of the Week on March 1.
  • Was perfect on extra points (7-for-7) and field goals (5-for-5), including a long of 39 yards against Trinity International (Ill.) of February 28.

Senior Josh Niego (Men's Basketball)

  • Was named All-Chicagoland Collegiate Athletic Conference (CCAC) in the Triple Jump at the CCAC Indoor Championships in Davenport, Iowa on February 19. He finished in second place with a distance of 13.07 meters.
  • He also earned All-CCAC in the Long Jump, placing third with a distance of 6.71 meters.

Senior Josh Niego (Men's Basketball)

  • Was the team leader in scoring, averaging 15.9 points per game in seven games.
  • Shot 50.6 percent from the field (41-for-81) and shot 53.6 percent (15-for-28) from beyond the arc.
  • Became the 42nd player in program history to score 1,000 or more career points against Trinity Christian (Ill.) on February 3.
