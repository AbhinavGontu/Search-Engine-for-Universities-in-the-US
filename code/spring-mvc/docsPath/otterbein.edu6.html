Social Distancing Guidelines

With the health and safety of our colleagues and guests as a top priority, we have integrated additional procedures to help prepare you for a safe and meaningful visit. These safety measures align with the Ohio Department of Health’s orders. Detailed instructions will be sent with your registration confirmation, but for now, please review the following:

  1. If you are experiencing symptoms of COVID-19, or recently tested positive for COVID-19, we ask that you take advantage of our virtual visit option until you have recovered. As a guide, please refer to our self-screening assessment tool (PDF).
  2. Students will be limited to bringing two guests with them as to not exceed a group limit of 10 individuals (including Admission staff members).
  3. To help protect our colleagues and guests, we are requiring the use of a face covering. If you are unable to fashion a face covering, one will be provided for you upon your arrival to campus. The face covering must cover your nose and mouth. In addition, we will be actively practicing the appropriate social distance throughout your visit.
  4. Hand sanitizer and disinfectant wipes will be available in multiple locations on campus.
  5. Students and families are required to register for a campus visit in order to participate in an on-campus visit experience. Again, students are limited to bringing two guests. Walk in appointments will not be accommodated at this time.
  6. Contactless check-in will be facilitated upon your arrival.

Virtual Visits for Admitted Students

Sign-up for a campus visit and schedule an appointment with your admission counselor. This will allow you to have a personalized tour to see the parts of campus you would like to get to know better. Then if you’d like, you may talk with your admission counselor to have your questions answered and to also learn about next steps in the process.

Register Now!

When scheduling a visit please make sure to check your confirmation email for a link to connect with faculty and other departments on campus such as:

  • Office of Disability Services/Academic Support Center – Discuss your IEP, learn about free tutoring services, and other services Otterbein offers that help students develop and strengthen the skills necessary to attain their academic goals.
  • Office of Student Success & Career Development – Learn how Otterbein supports its students with career exploration and advising, job search resources and strategies, grad school preparation, and other services to ensure continued support of your career and professional development needs.
  • Office of Student Financial Aid – Receive assistance with understanding the FAFSA as well as additional ways of paying for college, like loans and payment plans.
  • Study Abroad Programs – Find out what types of study abroad opportunities are available at Otterbein and how to make this option affordable to you.
  • Undecided Academic Advisor – Speak to an academic advisor who works specifically with students who come in as entering Freshman in an Undecided program to determine what support Otterbein offers to help students find their major.
  • ARMY ROTC – Learn about opportunities at Otterbein to obtain an officer’s commission in the United States Army and the many scholarship opportunities they provide. This meeting will be with an ROTC officer/instructor.

You can get to Otterbein by car, bus or airplane. And we know that once you’re here, you’ll want to stay.

Directions to Campus:

From the NORTH, take I-71 south to Gemini Place/IKEA Way/Exit 121. At the exit, turn left on Gemini Place, which becomes IKEA Way, then Worthington Road. Travel 1.5 miles. Turn right on Africa Road, which becomes West Street. Travel approximately 1.5 miles on Africa Road/West Street. Turn left on Main Street, travel .2 miles.

From the SOUTH on I-71, Route 315, or Route 23, take I-270 East (Wheeling) on the North side of Columbus. Follow I-270 to OH-3 north (exit 29) toward Westerville. Travel 2 miles and turn left on College Avenue. Travel .2 miles.

From the EAST or WEST on I-70, take I-270 North toward Cleveland. Travel 8 miles on I-270 and exit on OH-3 north (exit 29) toward Westerville. Travel 2 miles and turn left on College Avenue. Travel .2 miles.

By bus: Columbus is served by Greyhound Bus Lines. For information call (614) 221-2389.

By air: If you are flying, most major airlines service Columbus. The nearest major airport is Port Columbus International Airport, approximately 15 minutes from Westerville.

Visitor Parking Information

Visitors must contact the Police Department to obtain a visitor parking permit. The Police Department can be contacted in person at 194 West Main Street, by phone at 614-823-1222, or by e-mail at police@otterbein.edu. The visitor parking permit can be picked up in person during regular business hours, or e-mailed to an address provided.

Visitors may park in the Campus Center West Parking Lot, the Cowan Hall Parking Lot, the Psychology House Parking Lot, Davis Hall Parking Lot, 25 West Home Street Parking Lot, the Arts and Communication Building Parking Lot, and the Collegeview Center Parking Lot.

Citations issued for unregistered vehicles should be returned to the Otterbein University Police Department. They should be signed and dated by the visitor, explaining the situation. However, any citations issued for parking in fire lanes, handicapped spaces or otherwise illegal parking will be charged to that person.
