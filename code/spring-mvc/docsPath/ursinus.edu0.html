A Student’s Journey

Zeba Hussaini ’14

Zeba arrives as a biology major on the pre-med track.

Impressed by her welcome experience, Zeba decides to join the UC Ambassadors to pass on that comforting feeling to the next incoming class.
Zeba Hussaini ’14

Zeba becomes a fellow for the Parlee Center for Science and the Common Good

As an inaugural member, Zeba learns how to think about the connection between science and the humanities, and the impact this connection has on the world.
Zeba Hussaini ’14

Zeba performs in-depth research in cardiac function and dysfunction.

With med school within reach, Zeba strengthens her application with summer fellow research and year-long lab research on neurotransmitter release.
Zeba Hussaini ’14

Zeba finds inspiration in Applied Ethics coursework and creates major.

Studying alongside a cherished professor, Zeba is further exposed to the critical relationship of humanities and medicine.
Zeba Hussaini ’ 14

Zeba graduates and selects Jefferson Med to pursue her passion.

Attracted to their humanities program and focus on developing well-rounded clinicians, Zeba’s Parlee Center experience was a focal point in the med school interviews.
Zeba Hussaini ’14

Her passion for healthcare advocacy & ethics continues to Hershey Medical

In July 2021, following residency in internal medicine, Zeba will pursue a female gastroenterology fellowship, which, in her experience, has been a male dominated specialty.

Poe on Poe Now Streaming on Live on Main!

A five-part theatrical performance derived and inspired by the works of Edgar Allan Poe now available for viewing virtually. Poe on Poe, adapted and directed by Professor of Theater Domenick Scudera, brings Poe’s stories, poems, essays, and other writings to life, including The Tell-Tale Heart, The Raven, The Cask of the Amontillado, and more. The performances are filmed on stage in Ursinus College’s Kaleidoscope Performing Arts Center with five parts being released weekly.
