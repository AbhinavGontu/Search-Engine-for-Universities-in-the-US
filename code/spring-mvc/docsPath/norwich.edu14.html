Nothing teaches like EXPERIENCE. At Norwich University, whatever your major, whether your lifestyle (Corps of Cadets or civilian), opportunities to serve, learn and grow abound.

TO SERVE

Dedication to service is a Norwich hallmark; our students have delivered disaster relief after Tropical Storm Irene; boosted cybersecurity at a Super Bowl; designed life-improving gadgets for veterans and built homes for people with special needs. The world needs your strength, talent, grit and compassion more than ever. Read about our many volunteer opportunities.

Video: Flood Relief

TO LEAD

Our university motto is “I will try” but our ethos is “I will lead.” Norwich cultivates leadership in every aspect of student life, from Corps activities to ROTC field training to club leadership, from coordinating American Red Cross blood drives to supporting charities in need to leading your compadres on the field. Hear Erin Gatz ’17 share her secret to leadership.

Video: Rook Experience

READY

Norwich will prepare you to thrive in a career you love — in the private sector, public sector or U.S. armed forces. Some grads will pursue advanced degrees, including master’s degrees and doctorates. Read about Elizabeth Ells ’18, who parlayed engineering studies, athletics and a love of the ocean to pursue a doctorate at UMass Dartmouth’s School for Marine Science and Technology.

The Colleges and Schools within Norwich University

College of Liberal Arts

The College of Liberal Arts is home to four departments, one school and ten majors. Bachelor’s degrees are offered in Communications, Criminal Justice, English, History, International Studies, Political Science, Psychology, Spanish, Studies in War & Peace, and Education. The college also offers minors in Asian Studies, Computer Crime and Forensics, Transnational Crime, Chinese, French, German, Philosophy, Writing, and Sociology.

College of National Services

The College is comprised of the Departments of Army Military Science, Naval Science and Aerospace Science; each having a department chair and staff.

College of Professional Schools

The conceptual and hands-on meet in the College of Professional Schools.

Cross-discipline training, hands-on practice and our spirit of collaboration let students strengthen conceptual foundations and develop practical, real-world skills. Our graduates leave with the confidence, empathy and motivation to build the industries, processes and structures our evolving society needs.
