PVAMU :Coronavirus Update /CARES Act Student Relief Fund
CARES Act Student Relief Fundlslacy2021-01-28T00:45:47+00:00

Recently, the university was awarded approximately $6.9 million in Coronavirus Aid, Relief, and Economic Security (CARES) Act funding. The Act provides federal aid to institutions of higher education to help students cover expenses related to the disruption of campus operations due to COVID-19. Eligible expenses include areas such as food, housing, utilities, course materials, technology, healthcare, child care, and moving/transportation expenses.

Spring 2021 CARES Act Application Process

The spring 2021 application for CARES Act funds will be available on the CARES Act website beginning January 27, 2021. It will remain open until February 4, 2021. Disbursements to students will begin on February 8, 2021. To qualify for the funds, students must be enrolled in courses toward a PVAMU degree at the time of application and disbursement for Spring 2021, meet all eligibility requirements, have a 2020-2021 FAFSA on file with the university when the application is reviewed, and not have been enrolled in a fully online degree program prior to March 13, 2020. Any incomplete or inaccurate applications will not be considered. If you have any questions, please email us at pvcares@pvamu.edu.

Important Information: The CARES Act Funds CANNOT be used to cover any of the following expenses:

  • Anticipated expenses, such as credit card payments
  • Parking Tickets and other university-imposed fines
  • Non-essential utilities, (i.e. cable, phone)
  • Costs for entertainment, recreation, non-emergency travel (e.g. Study Abroad) or other non-essential expenses
  • Expenditures incurred as a result of poor financial management and not an emergency
  • Expenses needed for a family member or friend

CARES funds will be disbursed to you as a refund and will not be applied to your account balance. You are responsible for paying your bill.

Student Eligibility

To be a Title IV-eligible student under Section 484 of the HEA, the student must:

  • Be enrolled or accepted for enrollment in a degree, certificate or other recognized educational credential (including a program of study abroad approved for credit by the eligible institution at which such student is enrolled)–that is, a regular student under 34 CFR 600.2);
  • Not be enrolled in elementary or secondary school and have a high school diploma or its recognized equivalent;
  • Have satisfactory academic progress (SAP) if the student is a currently enrolled student;
  • Not owe an overpayment (refund) on Title IV grants and not be in default on a Title IV loan;
  • File with ED “as part of the original financial aid application process” a certification (Statement of Educational Purpose) that includes:
    • A statement of educational purpose, and
    • The student’s Social Security Number (SSN);
  • Be a U.S citizen or national, a permanent resident, or an eligible noncitizen;
  • Have returned any fraudulently obtained Title IV funds, if the student is convicted of or pled guilty or no contest to charges;
  • Not have fraudulently received Title IV loans in excess of annual or aggregate limits;
  • Have repaid any Title IV loan overpayment amounts in excess of annual or aggregate limits, if obtained inadvertently;
  • Have his Selective Service registration verified (the Title IV aid ineligibility for failure to register is actually in the Selective Service Act §3811(f));
  • Have a valid SSN, except for residents of the Federated States of Micronesia, Republic of the Marshall Islands, or the Republic of Palau; and
  • Not have a federal or state conviction for drug possession or sale, with certain time limitations.

CARES Act Fund Frequently Asked Questions

Am I eligible for CARES Act funds?

Current degree-seeking students who are enrolled in spring 2021, and who are eligible to apply for federal Title IV financial aid, are eligible to apply for CARES Act funds. Students enrolled in fully online programs prior to March 13, 2020, international, and undocumented students are not eligible. Funding is limited, and students are required to have completed the 2020-2021 Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA).

Are Graduate students eligible for CARES Act funding?

Yes. Both Undergraduate and Graduate students enrolled in the fall semester are eligible for funding.

Are there deadlines for applying for the CARES Act Emergency Grant funding?

Yes. The deadline for spring 2021 is February 4, 2021.

How can students apply for CARES Act funds?

Eligible students for spring 2021 will be able to apply online via a link on the CARES Act website: https://www.pvamu.edu/coronavirus/cares-act/.

How long before I know if I’m awarded?

Due to the large volume of applications, we work as quickly as possible to process submissions. When a decision to award funding is made, you will receive a notification.

How much funding will I get?

The maximum is $1,000 per student each semester.

How will I receive the funding if my request is approved?

Students will receive their funds based on the refund method they selected at the time of registration of classes.

I am an international student and not a citizen of the United States. Am I eligible for a CARES Act grant?

No. Because you are not able to apply for federal Title IV financial aid, you are not eligible.

I graduate this semester. Can I still receive assistance?

Yes. Graduating seniors are eligible to receive funding.

What are the types of expenses covered by CARES Act funding?

Eligible expenses for CARES Act funding include, but are not necessarily limited to, room and board, technology expenses, tuition and fees, childcare, healthcare, course materials, and other miscellaneous expenses included in the student’s cost of attendance.

Who do I contact if I have questions?

Please send an email to pvcares@pvamu.edu.
