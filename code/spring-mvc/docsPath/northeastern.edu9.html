A Community for All

Building a diverse and inclusive community is central to Northeastern’s mission because diversity of thought, culture, and viewpoint is essential to learning and growth.

Our Commitment

President Joseph E. Aoun has outlined the following series of actionable steps to advance justice and cultural literacy throughout our university community and to help end systemic racism.

The Addition of an NUPD Advisory Board

The board’s mission is to guide, inform, and strengthen NUPD’s relationship with the community—both within and outside Northeastern.

NUPD Advisory Board

Elevation of the Presidential Council on Diversity and Inclusion

The council, formed in 2013, has been charged with finding new opportunities for Northeastern to become more diverse, inclusive, and equitable, and to advance the cause of racial justice.

Presidential Council

Additional steps in the President’s Action Plan

  • Increase diversity and representation at all levels
  • Improve universitywide accountability
  • Appointed Robert Jose as Dean for Cultural and Spiritual Life
  • Improve support for Black students
  • Enact cultural competency and anti-racism training
  • Deepen community engagement
  • Amplify our impact through our employer partners

Ideas, Action, Change

Northeastern has long been focused on raising awareness and creating change toward a more equitable and socially just world.

John D. O’Bryant African American Institute

Supporting students of African origin and engaging the broader university community in developing student leaders.

Civil Rights and Restorative Justice Project

Helping scholars, policymakers, and organizers seek justice for civil rights crimes committed during the period of 1930 to 1970.

Center on Crime and Community Resilience

Finding innovative, research-based policy solutions to crime and other urban social problems.

Dukakis Center for Urban and Regional Policy

Developing and implementing policies to improve the quality of life and social justice in urban areas.

Institute on Race and Justice

Providing critical research to influence issues of race and inequality in the criminal justice and education systems.

Strategic Advancement of Rising Scholars (STARS) Fellowship

Offering funding and support to help underrepresented students of color pursue PhDs and careers in STEM.

Office of Institutional Diversity and Inclusion

This Northeastern office promotes the university’s commitment to equal opportunity, affirmative action, diversity, and social justice while building a climate of inclusion throughout our campuses.

She’s building a sense of campus community for Black women

Affinity groups such as Sisters in Solidarity provide Black women with a sense of belonging, says Kiera Perryman. The third-year design major joined the organization in her first year and welcomes the opportunity it provides for herself and others to see and be themselves.

Here are some books on the Black experience to add to your bookshelf

After a tumultuous 2020 plagued by COVID-19 and social unrest, people turned to books as sources of information and catharsis, says Melissa Pearson, assistant teaching professor in English at Northeastern. Here’s a comprehensive reading list, composed of books from The New York Times Best Sellers list, Amazon charts, and Snell Library’s Anti-Racism Guide, to learn more about Black experiences.

For these students, personal stories and respect are key to promoting racial literacy

Northeastern students shared deeply personal stories about the impact of racism throughout their lives—describing schoolyard taunts and uncomfortable conversations with family members—as part of an ongoing discussion about racial literacy.
