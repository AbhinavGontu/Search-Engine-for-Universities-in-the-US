Spring 2021

View the University’s plan for Spring 2021 

Top things to know - Updated March 16, 2021

  • The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention recommends that you defer travel if possible, but that if you do travel:
    • You know the testing requirements for your destination, and for returning, even if traveling domestically. Many states have current or recent quarantine restrictions that could continue throughout the spring.
    • You test before leaving and test again five to seven days after returning.
    • For international travel, remember that all air passengers arriving in the United States, including U.S. citizens, are required to have a negative COVID-19 test result or documentation of recovery from COVID-19 before they board their flight.
  • University employees are eligible to be tested at the MTest site at RecWell. Online scheduling is available at the MyBoynton portal. If you are unable to log in to the portal please call 612-625-3222.
  • While vaccine allocation to the University varies week-to-week (i.e., limited to none), the state of Minnesota has multiple programs aimed at connecting Minnesotans with the vaccine. The state’s Vaccine Connector will help people find out when, where, and how to get a COVID-19 vaccine. To share your interest with the state of Minnesota, sign up here.
  • The restriction on all international travel for University business by students, faculty, and staff has been extended to April 30, 2021. However, faculty, staff, and graduate students may apply for an exception to the restriction.
  • All employees who can work from home should continue doing so through at least August 2, 2021. If you need to return to campus sooner than August 2 to conduct your work, please work with your supervisor to coordinate a return and to update your unit’s sunrise plan as needed.
  • Sunrise Plan: Twin Cities faculty, staff, postdoctoral students, and graduate assistants should follow the unit-level process to request to return to their worksite. This includes a new form to make the request. Additionally, University daytime summer camps, activities, events and recreation centers can apply to resume operations. Guidance for department in-person, on-campus events can be found here.
