Community Engagement

Campus-Community Partnerships

carnegie_cec_digital_seal.png

The Office of Community Engagement seeks to engage members of the broader community and the University of Minnesota, Morris students, faculty and staff in reciprocal, meaningful course-based and co-curricular partnerships.

Partnership mission

  • Meet identified community needs
  • Advance the campus mission, learning outcomes and key campus priorities
  • Work toward more vibrant, just and inclusive communities

Programs Available

Community-Engaged Learning
Plan, implement, and assess service-learning projects that fit course goals and meet documented community needs.

Community Building Programs
Community Meal, Soup and Substance, Gather in the Park, and Bread 'N Bowls are opportunities to eat together and build community.

Tutoring, Reading, and Empowering Children (TREC)
Provides UMM students with opportunities to tutor and mentor children in pre-K through Grade 6

Employment and Leadership Opportunities
Training, reflection, and community-building opportunities for students interested in taking a leadership role

Morris Intercultural Education Initiative
Partnerships with our regional Latino community.

Days of Service
Opportunities to build community by serving together in short-term projects.
