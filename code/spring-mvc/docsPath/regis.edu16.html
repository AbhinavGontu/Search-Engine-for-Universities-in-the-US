Stay Connected

The Regis Archives and Special Collections ePublications include full access to yearbooks, bulletins and catalogs, the Highlander, the Brown and Gold, and alumni publications. Regis University alumni are encouraged to stay in touch with the library and are welcome to visit the Dayton Memorial Library in person to use print and online resources. Guest logins for using library databases are available whenever a librarian is on duty; please check the current research help desk schedule.

Access to Regis Library Resources

Due to licensing agreements alumni do not have remote access to Regis subscription databases, but there are ways to find other free scholarly online resources. The tabs on this guide will connect you to free databases, online journals, government websites, and statistics websites in specific subject areas.

Alumni may also check out up to six books and/or media materials at a time as a special borrower.

The handouts below will give more detailed instructions for some of the resources that are included in the guide and help you to find out if you can obtain remote online access to databases and online journals from your local public library.

  • How to Find Articles in the Directory of Open Access Journals (DOAJ)
    This tutorial demonstrates how to find articles in DOAJ.
  • How to Use Google Scholar to Find Scholarly Articles

Access to Databases & Online Journals From Your Local Public Library

You may be surprised to learn what your local public library offers, which might include some of the databases you used as a Regis student. Your public library card number might allow you to log in to the databases and online journals from the library's website. 

Use the directory at www.publiclibraries.com to find your public library's web site and links to its online resources. If your library is not listed, contact the Research Help Desk using the contact information to the right for assistance.

  • Connecting to your Public Library's Online Resources
    This tutorial gives examples using health care topics but they apply equally to other fields using the Academic Search Premier and Business Source Premier databases. These databases will link you to full-text online journals.
