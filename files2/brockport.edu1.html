The Days That Made a Difference

It's hard to choose just one day, but I do remember the second Saturday of my freshman year, September of 1996, meeting a couple of my very best friends. Our group is still very close. It doesn't matter if we all chat once a day, week, month or year; it always feels like no time has passed.
- Keri Vacanti '00
The day when I received notes of appreciation from our alumni saying that I had a positive impact on their lives and also the day when I received the College's Presidential Teaching Excellence Award. These events made me realize that what I do on a daily basis isn't just a job; it's a privilege and an honor to be able to spend time with our students, to empower, to encourage, and to challenge.
- Chair and Associate Professor of Computing Sciences Mehruz Kamal
The day at Brockport that made the most difference in my life was the last day of classes my senior year. As the president of Brockport Student Government, my staff and I worked hard to host the last "Brock the Port." This was an important day, because it was the culmination of all our hard work, but also it affirmed my interest in pursuing a career in higher education and student affairs.
- Kevin Perry '12
The most important was my first day on campus in August of 1994. I was getting prepared for my first college football preseason. I remember being so nervous and not sure what to expect. It was very challenging and difficult to be an athlete and a student but just conquering that fear and learning how to have grit that my coaches and teammates taught me has made all the difference.
- Ryan Lanigan '98
It wasn't until Homecoming three years ago that I really felt like I was fully home at Brockport. I brought my kids for the parade and to hang out for the day. Not only was everyone excited to see me there with the kids (they're very cute, I get it), but the students I had met took us all under their wings. At that point, I had loved the possibility of what my role at Brockport could be, but after that day, I just loved Brockport.
- Director of the Academic Success Center Thomas Chew
One day I went into Career Services and learned about the Disney College program. I knew I was graduating a semester early, so I thought with all the internship opportunities I had done through the Delta College program working at Disney would be cool. Years later here I am. Iâ€™ve worked in all four theme parks, one resort, and three different roles.
- Judy Fixler '16
I was a freshman walking through the student union, and for whatever reason, this bright red piece of paper taped against the wall caught my eye. It was an ad for a recruitment meeting for 89.1 The Point. Mind you, getting into radio was not something that I thought would be a career move for me. That led me to now a 14-year career in radio, six of it back here in Rochester. I can only thank Brockport and The Point for that.
- Corey James '06 of 98 PXY
The day at Brockport that made the most difference in my life was the day I was hired. I joined a program with a rich tradition in history and Athletics, and I became a member of the family.
- Athletic Director Erick Hart
I came to Brockport looking to find a sense of academic direction. One of my professors could sense that I was having trouble and, in a 1:1 setting, offered very realistic, relatable advice. He explained that no matter who puts what pressure on me, if it doesnâ€™t feel right, then it probably isnâ€™t right. Keeping that in mind, I found something that felt right for me. And it still does!
- Jack Distefano '17
The day at Brockport that made the most difference in my life was ironically the first day I attended Brockport. From this day forward, I got involved in so many different things on campus, BSG, Honors College, and so much more. This day was really so impactful for me, because it shaped my future as a student at Brockport and now as an active alum.
- Tori McLaughlin '20
The day at Brockport that made the most difference in my life was volunteering for our Undergraduate Commencement ceremony. Commencement is a big day for any graduate, the culmination of years of hard work, a celebration of pride, and a dream fully realized, especially for our first-generation college students. And for many of them, that goal was realized with the help of the Brockport Foundation.
- Assistant Director of Undergraduate Admissions Megan Sarkis
The day I met my future wife in Harmon Hall in the spring of 1997, the day I made the Golden Eagles baseball team, the day I graduated and was first in my family to earn a college degree, the day I broke the strikeout record at Brockport on my last game as a Golden Eagle. These are just a few of the special days that make my experience at SUNY Brockport so meaningful.
- Engagement Officer Adam Standish '98/'00
The most meaningful, life-changing day at SUNY Brockport was when I became an active member of Zeta Phi Beta Sorority, Inc.
- Lisa Fleming-Benjamin '97
The first day I started work at Brockport has made a huge difference in my life!
- Multimedia Producer Meghan Finnerty
When I worked as the College's Sports Information Director, I got the opportunity to go on a donor visit with Brad Schreiber to talk about a major gift for our baseball and softball complex with Clark and Fran Whited. We ended up getting the gift that put their name on the complex and I, soon after, transitioned from sports and PR to alumni and fundraising.
- Vice President for Advancement Mike Andriatch '85/'17
The day at Brockport that made the biggest difference in my life was the first time that I ever reported in front of the camera. Up until this point, I had always been behind the scenes. I thought that's what I wanted to, but I was in a class where I had to do a one-minute live shot on camera. That changed the path of my career, and I'm always going to remember that day as me learning something new that I never thought that I could ever do, and it led me to where I am today.
- Kayla Green '18
The very first day at Brockport was the game changer for me. It was the day that changed my life and basketball career. I found a new home. I will always love the Port. They helped me become the man I am today. Forever #EagleNation.
- John Ivy '16
My most life-changing day at Brockport took place the second week of my freshman year in 2007. The day that made the biggest impact on me during my time at Brockport was when the community came together to raise over $1,000 to fly me down to Texas for Brockport when they played against Mary Hardin-Baylor in the Final Four in the football college playoff. Really awesome experience, one of the coolest things I've done, met some amazing people along the way. It showed how much the community really cares about people.

The day at Brockport that made the most difference in my life was the day that professors took a chance on me, to help me with my studies if I needed so I could obtain my degree. Also friends that I made at Brockport that had stood by me all these years later.
