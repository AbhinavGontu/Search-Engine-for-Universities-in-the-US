York at a Glance

Your choice of college has enormous impact on your future and your present.
Its a decision best made with both your head and your heart.

York College. Where talents and passions are identified and nurtured. Where real-world experience, close connections and personal growth are emphasized. Where students are challenged to become the best versions of themselves. Where the value of a good education is proven, every day. From day one.

Apply Give to YCP

What makes York College special? A mix of incredible people and heartfelt traditions, to start.York Traditions

Commencement
These graduation ceremonies are held in the winter and spring and date back to 1874, making it the oldest campus tradition. Dressed in academic regalia - the gown, hood and cap - students are presented with their degrees. The ceremonies usually include thoughts from a faculty speaker and an honorary degree recipient.
Fall Fest and Homecoming Weekend
Each October, the York College community invites alumni, families, parents and the community to campus for a weekend celebration.
Old Spart
Our beloved Old Spart, located beside the Bookstore, shows off autographs from each graduating class. Seniors sign it right after Commencement, with a fresh coat of paint applied to provide a blank canvas. Always a favorite spot for photos!
Kente Service
A beautiful Kente service is held for upcoming graduates through our Intercultural Student Life and Global Programming Office. A fabric native to Ghana, kente is a visual representation of the wearer's history, philosophy, ethics, oral literature, religious beliefs, social values and political thoughts.
Senior Class Gift
York College seniors have the opportunity to help their fellow students and begin their philanthropic legacy. The Senior Class Gift isn't just about fundraising. It's about preserving the traditions that make a York College experience unique.

Fast FactsA look around York College

  • Academics
    • Masters degrees in accounting, business administration, education, integrated marketing communication, leadership, music industry studies, nursing and public policy and administration;
    • Doctoral degree in nursing practice
    • Offers seminars, workshops, and academic experiences that prepare you for professional success.
    • All qualified students are guaranteed experience-based learning opportunities.
    • Most popular majors: nursing, biology, education, business, psychology, sport management, criminal justice, and engineering
  • Campus
    • 190-acre suburban campus
    • Adjacent to York, population 45,000
    • 45-minute drive from Harrisburg; 1-hour drive from Baltimore; two-and -hour drive from Philadelphia; two-hour drive from D.C.; three-and--hour drive from New York City, Richmond or Pittsburgh; four-hour drive from Long Island
  • Financial Information
    • 2018-19 costs: Tuition and fees (annual) $20,100 room and board (typical) $11,200. That's about $13,000 less for tuition than the average private school in Pennsylvania! And yet York College still can provide nationally-acclaimed academic programs, class sizes and access to faculty that public universities can't touch, and the kind of career preparation coupled with a well-rounded academic program that puts you in the best possible position for success. That's real value. That's what York College of Pennsylvania is in a class of its own.
    • Full-tuition scholarships are offered through the Presidential Research Fellowship and York College Community Opportunity Scholarship Program. Specific scholarship opportunities are available for transfers and for international students as well.
    • More than 90% of incoming freshmen receive financial aid or scholarships.
    • The average resident freshmen financial aid award is $19,214. We value students who put in the work in high school needed to be successful. We value students who want to be around others who push them to be their best. And our scholarships and grants back that up.
  • What Others Say
    • Ranked in the top tier of the Best Regional Universities in the North [U.S. News & World Report Best Colleges]. U.S. News also ranked York College as one of the Best Regional Universities in the North for Veterans, Engineering programs, and a Best Value School. 
    • Best Value ranked York, Pennsylvania as having one of the most charming college town main streets in the country.
    • Named a 2019-20 College of Distinction for Business, Education, Engineering, Nursing and for all of Pennsylvania.
    • Ranked among the top 50 Hospitality and Tourism and Public Relations by Study.com.
    • Nursing Schools Almanac lists York College in the top 15 in mid-Atlantic for its nursing program.
    • One of the 50 Best Value Colleges for a Nursing Degree by College Factual.
    • Best Bachelor's In Sports Administration Degree Programs for 2019 by Value Colleges.
    • Recognized as one of the best RN programs in Pennsylvania by RegisteredNursing.org. 
    • Central Penn Business Journal lists York College's MBA program as one of the best. 
    • Zippia named York College's a top 10 college for design and visual communication majors in Pennsylvania.
    • Phi Theta Kappa Honor Society recognizes York College on the 2019 Transfer Honor Roll. 
    • RN Careers lists York College as a Best RN Program in Pennsylvania.
  • Classic College Feel
    • 23 NCAA Division III sports teams; member of Capital Athletic Conference
    • 30+ intramural/club sports, such as dance, softball, baseball, men's lacrosse and more
    • 100+ student clubs and organizations
    • 11 Greek life organizations
    • Residential college with nearly 60% of students residing in College-owned housing
    • 4,000 full-time undergraduate students; 300 graduate students
    • 172 full-time faculty; 91% hold the highest degree possible in their field
  • History
    • York Academy received its charter from the General Assembly of Pennsylvania in 1787
    • The City of York was the first capital of the United States, birthplace of the Articles of Confederation, and where the words The United States of America were first spoken.

Where is York College?

York College is located in York, PA, in the heart of the jobs-rich mid-Atlantic. York is in the midst of an exciting revitalization with small businesses and entrepreneurial creatives leading the way.
  • 45 minutes from Harrisburg
  • One hour from Baltimore
  • Two hours from D.C.
  • Two and a half hours from Philadelphia
  • Three and a half hours from New York City, Pittsburgh, or Richmond
