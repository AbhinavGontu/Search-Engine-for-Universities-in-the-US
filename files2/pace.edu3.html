Neil S. Braun Student Mentoring Program

In the spirit of Setters helping Setters, the Neil S. Braun Student Mentoring Program connects current students with Pace alumni to offer everything from life hacks to in-depth career advice to networking in the industry they want to someday break into. Pairs can meet and chat through whatever medium suits them best: e-mail, phone, direct message (DM), or even in person—all it takes is that initial "hello".

A key component of the University’s Pace Path initiative is student mentoring, and so the Neil S. Braun Student Mentoring Program is designed to help all students by connecting them with the Pace alumni community who want to stay involved and give back. Alumni mentors serve as guides for student mentees so they are able to achieve both long and short-term goals. At the same time, alumni mentors contribute to the professional and personal development of students and the academic community as a whole.

Program Overview

Use this vital program to:

  • Choose your own mentor
  • Get the insider track on the field of your choice
  • Step out of your comfort zone and stretch yourself by exploring new career paths
  • Learn interviewing skills and business etiquette
  • Build your professional network with Pace alumni worldwide
  • Connect with your chosen mentor over coffee, by phone, video chat - whatever the two of you decide
  • Alumni inspire, motivate, and support current students by joining the program!

Students — why you should choose a mentor

Your mentor is a Pace alumni and can share insight on how a Pace education translates into the professional or graduate-level academic world. Through this program, you can obtain guidance through your life at Pace and toward your eventual career. Working one-on-one, your alumni mentor can help motivate you, boost your self-confidence and key you in about internships and career opportunities. Mentorship at Pace can establish a partnership and network you draw on for life. Additionally, the program is flexible enough to work within your comfort zone and around your availability. Here are some options:

  • Long-term mentoring: go in depth — meet one-on-one from a single-semester to a year-long term
  • Flash mentoring: meet once to address a key issue, such as doing an informational interview for a class assignment
  • Fast mentoring: do a “mentoring-intensive” over a limited time period (30 days, for example). Get insight on careers or even “shadow” your mentor at his or her job for a day. 

Alumni — why you should become a mentor

There’s an old saying that goes: “the best way to really learn something is to teach it.” By sharing what you know with a Pace student, you’ll gain a deeper appreciation for your own profession — while helping a fellow alumnus succeed. You will also:

  • Expand your own network by meeting bright new minds
  • Enhance your leadership and communications skills
  • Get inspired through sharing ideas and brainstorming
  • Learn what motivates the coming generation of professionals
  • Give back to the Pace community in a meaningful way


Requirements

Students: To fully benefit from the Neil S. Braun Student Mentoring Program, you’ll need to bring your motivation to the relationship. That includes being proactive about keeping the conversation going! Also, don’t select a mentor by profession (or by school) alone. Many have a range of skills wider than their current job descriptions. Other requirements:

  • You must be currently enrolled and taking courses at Pace
  • Communicate regularly and be responsive to your mentor
  • Log-in at least weekly and become familiar with the platform
  • Follow the Pace Code of Conduct
  • Enroll here

Mentors: Mentors are the gatekeepers to the future. We are looking for Pace Alumni from a whole range of industries and professions. If you enjoy one-to-one communication and the opportunity share what you’ve learned, please join us. Requirements:

  • You are currently employed or recently retired (within the past 3 years)
  • Available for regular contact with mentee
  • Willing to provide feedback to program administrators
  • Complete the enrollment form found here
