Dru Fitz of the University of Jamestown is this weeks GPAC/Hauff Mid-America Sports pitcher-of-the-week. Fitz, a senior from Gresham, Oregon, tossed a complete game three-hit shutout in Jamestown's 4-0 win at Northwestern. He set a career high with 11 strikeouts and did not issue a walk while improving his record to 3-0 this season.
Kat Miska of the University of Jamestown is this weeks GPAC/Hauff Mid-America Sports pitcher-of-the-week. Miska, a junior from Pequot Lakes, Minnesota, was 2-0 with a 0.58 ERA in two starts for Jamestown last week. She tossed a complete game 2-1 win over second-ranked Science & Arts (Okla.), with nine strikeouts. In addition, she struck out 12 in five innings in an 8-2 win over Bethany while improving her record to 6-1 with a 0.48 ERA, including 89 strikeouts in 44 IP and an opponent batting average of .139 on the season.
Morningsides Lexie Stolen is this weeks GPAC/Hauff Mid-America Sports softball player-of-the-week. Stolen, a senior from Sioux City, Iowa, extended her current hit streak to nine games as the Mustangs went 8-0 on a tour of the Kansas Collegiate Athletic Conference. She concluded the week with five straight multiple-hit contests. In all she crushed four home runs and scored 14 runs to take national lead in both categories.
Concordias Jayden Adams is this weeks GPAC/Hauff Mid-America Sports baseball player-of-the-week. Adams, a freshman from Waverly, Nebraska, went 11-for-19 (.579) with 11 runs scored, three doubles, two triples, 13 RBIs and a .947 slugging percentage during that stretch. The second baseman helped Concordia to a 5-0 record for the week. On the season, Adams is hitting .566 with 12 extra base hits, 27 runs and 24 RBIs.
