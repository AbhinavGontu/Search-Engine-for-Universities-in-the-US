Spring 2021 Updates

  • COVID-19 vaccines and the coming 2021-22 academic year: With regard to the COVID-19 vaccines, UC's physician-led COVID Response Team advocates for vaccination as a safe and a significant contributor to the safety of our community. Thus, the university strongly recommends COVID-19 vaccination but will not mandate COVID-19 vaccination for students, faculty or staff for the coming academic year. Find links related to the COVID-19 vaccine and its planned distribution in Ohio.
  • Spring 'Know Before You Go' testing is offered mid-April: For peace of mind, to contribute to public health efforts, and to help you and your families take any added health precautions, the university will provide free, voluntary COVID testing Monday and Tuesday, April 12-13; and Monday and Tuesday, April 19-20 to any currently enrolled student in spring “Know Before You Go” testing. 
  • Commencement: The university will host two in-person Spring 2021 Commencement ceremonies on Thursday, April 29, and Friday, April 30, in historic Nippert Stadium for Spring 2021 graduates.
  • Campus planning is underway for Fall 2021 and calls for increased on-campus and in-person activity as well as expanded opportunities for in-person learning, teaching, staffing and campus life while keeping the safety and health of our students, faculty, staff and visitors paramount.

Questions

Please read the above information, links and other navigation on this page.  If you still have questions after reviewing the available information, please email publichealth@uc.edu. Latest News

Spectrum News: UC students create 3D-printed face masks

March 16, 2021

Spectrum News 1 talks to UC College of Engineering and Applied Science students about their custom 3D-printed face masks created through a phone app scan of your face.

ABC News: Impact of pandemic cancer screening pause

March 16, 2021

Months after mammograms, colonoscopies and other routine cancer screenings, like lung cancer screenings, were suspended because of the pandemic, researchers are studying the impact.

Infectious Disease Advisor: COVID-19 Vasculitis or Novel...

March 15, 2021

COVID-19 can lead to inflammation of the blood vessels in the lungs and other key organs, says Richard Becker, MD, a UC cardiologist and director of the UC Heart, Lung and Vascular Institute. Vasculitis is a contributor to acute respiratory syndrome in COVID-19 patients.

Medscape: New adjuvant boosts SARS-CoV-2 protection in animal...

March 15, 2021

Carl Fichtenbaum, MD, of the UC College of Medicine Division of Infectious Diseases, was featured as an expert source in a story on Medscape about animal model research showing that a new adjuvant, or ingredient, added to COVID-19 vaccines has shown promise for its ability to deliver the vaccine directly into lymph nodes.

WVXU: COVID-19, lung and brain images and stroke risk

March 15, 2021

University of Cincinnati researchers say for the first time they have a visual correlation between the severity of COVID-19 in the lungs and the effect on patients' brains, WVXU-FM reports.

New UC telehealth drone makes house calls

March 15, 2021

Three colleges at the University of Cincinnati collaborated to develop a semi-autonomous drone that can be dispatched right to people’s homes. The drones are big enough to carry medicine or medical supplies but small enough to maneuver the tight confines of a home using navigational algorithms developed by UC engineers so patients can talk face to face to their doctors or pharmacists.
