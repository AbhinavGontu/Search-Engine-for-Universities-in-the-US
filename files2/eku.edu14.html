Campaign News

2020 Foundation Report Out Now
Check out our newest foundation report to see an update on the funds we raised this year, the number of students supported via foundation scholarships, and the ways we were able to support the overall success of the University through donations. Read stories of how donor gifts have impacted our students and why donors choose to give back to EKU. Enjoy the report!
Thanks for the Memories: Genevieve Clay

“Thanks for the memories.” It was Bob Hope’s theme song, and it’s Genevieve Clay’s motivation as a long-time supporter of the EKU Libraries. Both Genevieve and her late husband, EKU professor Dr. William Wilson Hume Clay, found EKU Libraries to be a worthy investment.

Scholarship, Conference Room Named for Gibson, Jr.
Dr. Gibson served EKU as the dean of the graduate school program from 1973 to 1985, promoting the benefits of graduate work and ensuring students were supported in the next step of their educational careers. Dr. Gibson lived his life intentionally, always seeking to help others and make life better for those around him.
Record EKU Foundation Scholarships Power Opportunity
This past fiscal year, at a time when many students and families have been negatively impacted by the COVID-19 pandemic, the University awarded 1,797 Foundation scholarships valued at a total of $1,303,360, both eclipsing previous records set the previous year.
A Legacy of Learning: Dr. Stuart Tobin

In 1977, he was driving from Georgia, where he was stationed in the military, to apply for a job in Lexington. He saw a sign for Eastern Kentucky University along I-75 and decided to explore. He has stayed since. In Richmond, Dr. Tobin found a shining example of a small, friendly Kentucky town bolstered by a major educational institution at its heart.

Maggard Endows Scholarship for Letcher County
Ed Maggard and Ann, his wife of 46 years, want youth from Letcher County who just need a "leg up", to succeed as he did and to dream what is possible. They established the Ed and Ann Maggard Letcher County Endowed Scholarship fund in 2019, and have recently fully funded the endowment totaling $25,000 in donations.
Annual Giving Fall 2020 Impact Report Out Now!
The 2020 Annual Giving Impact Report is now available! Read to learn about how donor support has made education more affordable and opportunities more abundant at Eastern Kentucky University over the past year.
Former Athlete Coaching Business Students: Jim Guice
Jim Guice, ’69, enjoyed an impressive college football career at Eastern under legendary coach Roy Kidd. Now, as a top executive for transportation company Corcentric, he is investing in the future careers of College of Business and Technology students while honoring the legacy of his former coach.
Colonel Couple Make a Return on Their EKU Investment: Don and April Perry

At EKU, Don Perry, ’86 ’95, and April Russell Perry, ’86 ’96, met, fell in love and gained an education that enabled them to excel in the banking industry. Now, with a generous gift to the banking and financial services program, they hope to help students find similar success.

Colonel Roots Run Deep: Dr. John Rowlett
Dr. John Rowlett, the third child and now a physician in Savannah, Georgia, established two endowments at his alma mater. The first, the Dr. John D. Rowlett Endowed Fund for Faculty Scholarship, Research, or Professional Development, honors the work of his late father. More recently, Dr. Rowlett honored his mother by establishing the Mary Anne Mason Rowlett Endowed Scholarship for deserving young women from Mom’s native Bourbon County.
Whitakers Support the Future of EKU Through Planned Gift

The Whitaker family and philanthropy seem to go hand in hand. Dr. Donald R. and Dr. Sue H. Whitaker have built an enormous legacy, donating to create endowed scholarships at four Universities, each supporting a variety of students in their pursuit of higher education. EKU is fortunate to be included in this list of Universities, further enabling financial support of students through the generosity of Donald and Sue.

Rewarding Veteran Service: Randy and Sharon Shaw

Randy Shaw, ’79, got a leg up at Eastern. Now, he and his wife Sharon want to offer the same for other veterans enrolled in the on-campus Occupational Health and Safety program with the Randy and Sharon Shaw Scholarship. Shaw understands the challenges veterans face while going back to school firsthand.

Hard Work Pays Off: Dr. Scott Arias
Dr. Scott Arias is passionate about rewarding students who show the same type of work ethic as his late father-in-law, George Blanka. That’s why Arias, a former director of the EKU construction management program, has endowed the George Blanka Construction Management Endowed Scholarship, named for the man who helped him start his million-dollar construction consulting company, ACE Consulting.
Former Faculty Give Back: Martha Luy
Martha Luy and her late husband, Jack, served EKU for decades as faculty members. After retirement, Martha continues to show her pride through her generous support of EKU Libraries and scholarships for nursing students.
Filling a Financial Gap: Judith Mills
EKU strives to create opportunity for students who are low-income and first-generation — but many students outside those categories also struggle to fund a college education. That’s why Judith Mills established the Drs. S. M. and Judith A. Mills and James Mills Endowed Scholarship Fund.
Champion of Education: Andrew Page
Andrew “Champ” Page, ’92, excelled as a track athlete during his time at EKU. Now, he excels in the business world as chief accounting officer for Advance Auto Parts. He credits his success in large part to his Eastern Experience.
“Make No Little Plans” Campaign Launched

Eastern Kentucky University’s public phase of its comprehensive fundraising campaign launched at a gala at the University Center for the Arts on Friday, Sept. 27 with three new pledges totaling nearly $5 million. Entitled “Make No Little Plans-The Campaign for EKU,” the goal was announced as $50 million by EKU Foundation Board Chair, and campaign chair Maribeth Berman. Berman announced that $39 million has already been committed.

Campus Revitalization: Dr. Geri Polvino
New and renovated athletics facilities, residence halls, classroom buildings and green spaces have drastically changed the landscape. Women's volleyball coach Geri Polvino committed $100,000 to the University to fund a comprehensive renovation to the women’s volleyball locker room in Alumni Coliseum.
Battelle Gifts to Support Research in Science Totals $70,000
Battelle, a global nonprofit research and development organization, continues to make significant investments in the undergraduate research program in the College of Science at EKU. Battelle's gifts over the last three years total $70,000.
First EKU Giving Day a Big Success
Eastern Kentucky University’s first-ever giving day was a record-setting success, bringing in far more donations in 24 hours than in any other single day in the University’s history. Giving Day brought in 750 donations from every U.S. state and Puerto Rico, totaling more than $125,000.
Foundation Sets Record Numbers for Scholarships
The Eastern Kentucky University Foundation, through its donors, is providing more opportunities than ever before for EKU students. The Foundation awarded 1,366 scholarships totaling more than $1.125 million for the 2017-2018 academic year.
Chi Omega Presents Stained-Glass Windows to EKU
Chi Omega will celebrate its 50th anniversary at EKU next year. To mark the occasion, the sorority will gift two intricate stained-glass windows to the University. In the course of a year, the sorority’s 1,000 EKU alumni donated nearly $25,000 to fund the creation of the windows.
Novelis Gift Supports Science and Math Camp
For the second consecutive year, Novelis, the leading producer of flat-rolled aluminum products and the world’s largest recycler of aluminum, has made a $7,000 gift in support of the Science and Mathematics Summer Camp program in the College of Science at Eastern Kentucky University.
Science Building Now Boasts 120 Solar Modules
EKU’s new Science Building now has 120 solar modules to help the campus increase its renewable energy supply. This was enabled by a generous donation from EKU alumnus Dr. Gary Booth, demonstrating his ongoing commitments to Eastern students and to environmental stewardship.
Capital Campaign Running Ahead of Pace
Eastern Kentucky University is ahead of schedule as it nears the public phase of its current capital campaign. The public phase of the campaign, titled “Make No Little Plans,” launches September 27, 2019.
Newest Foundation Professors Honored
Dr. Stephanie McSpirit and Dr. Charlotte Rich have earned the 2018-20 EKU Foundation Professorship, the highest honor for teaching excellence at Eastern Kentucky University.
Battelle Gift Supports Undergraduate Research
Battelle, a nonprofit research and development organization, presented a $20,000 check to the EKU College of Science to support the undergraduate research program.
Battelle Gift Supports Ky. Science Fair
Battelle, an Ohio-based nonprofit research and development organization, presented a $5,000 community grant check to support the 16th annual Kentucky Science and Engineering Fair.
Cupboard Fights Hunger on Campus, in Community
The Colonel's Cupboard designed to aid students experiencing food insecurity is now serving the community. The Cupboard, aided by Student Life and First-Year Experiences staff, recently donated approximately 500 pounds of food to God’s Outreach in Richmond.
Bechtel Parsons Supports EHS Program
John A.D. McArthur, environmental manager of Bechtel Parsons Blue Grass, recently visited EKU to meet with students and to present their second check to the Department of Environmental Health Science.
Former Student Regent Establishes Scholarship
Former student regent and Student Government Association president, Ritchie Rednour, has pledged $100,000 to establish the Ritchie Rednour Endowed Scholarship. This scholarship is used to aid students in their final semester.
Gift Supports Women in RMI Program
Thanks to a $25,000 donation from the Women Leaders in Insurance and Financial Services (WLI) to the EKU Risk Management and Insurance Program, women have additional help in reaching their educational and career goals.
Novelis Gifts Support STEM Camp
Novelis Aluminum Corporation donated $7,000 to support a science, technology, engineering and mathematics (STEM) summer camp in the College of Science at Eastern Kentucky University.
Turners Pledge $1 Million to Alma Mater
As chair of the Eastern Kentucky University Board of Regents the past four years, Craig Turner knows firsthand that the need for private support of the institution has never been greater.
Another Record Year for Private Support
Scholarships that support the University’s commitment to student success and result in improved retention and graduation rates. A helping hand for underrepresented minority students. An innovative green energy initiative at the New Science Building. An endowed professorship in music.
