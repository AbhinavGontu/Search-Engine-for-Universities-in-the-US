Author's 1st published story wins national award
Colette Bancroft, English '74, was awarded the Robert L. Fish Memorial Award for best story by a previously unpublished American author. Her story, "The Bite," appears in the new anthology, "Tampa Bay Noir." 
Announcing the 2021 USF Outstanding Young Alumni!
Six accomplished Bulls aged 35 years or younger comprise the newest class of Outstanding Young Alumni. They include a former NFL player; a two-time Latin GRAMMY winner; a two-time Emmy Award-winning social media producer for Good Morning America; a leading USAF intelligence officer; an engineer facilitating health care in Africa; and an acclaimed assistant city attorney. 
Alumni-led petition leads to popular new course on racism
A Change.org petition posted in June by Valentina Acosta, '18, and Nicholas Stewart, '20, has led to a popular new USF class: "Prejudice, Stigma and Race Consciousness," developed and taught by Alexis Mootoo, '10, MA '12 and PhD '17.
Music alum takes home his 2nd Latin GRAMMY
Jose Valentino Ruiz, ’09 and PhD ’16, has won a second Latin GRAMMY, this time for Best Classical Contemporary Composition for Sacre, from the Lignarius Album.
