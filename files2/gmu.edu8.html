Alumni in Action: Xavier Clark
Xavier Clark, MPA ’18, certainly has a full schedule as a senior strategist at the Department of Homeland Security, a communications PhD student in Mason’s College of Humanities and Social Sciences, an adjunct instructor in the communications department, a communication manager at the Fashion For All Foundation, and fashion blogger. Through it all, Xavier credits Mason with preparing them to be bold in the opportunities that come their way.
 
Student activist lights the way toward a more inclusive Mason
Dominique Dowling, a junior in the Integrative Studies program, aspires to be a future U.S. secretary of education. As an active student on campus and in the university's NAACP chapter, she’s not new to advocating for social justice. She's using her experience to help improve Mason by joining more than 100 faculty, staff, and students working on the university’s Anti-Racism and Inclusive Excellence Task Force.
 
Alumna is hospitals' first line of COVID defense
In the wake of COVID-19, epidemiology has emerged as central to a functioning society. Saskia Popescu, PhD ’19, utilizes her expertise to help hospitals, governments, and businesses to develop effective public health measures. Popescu, a Schar School of Policy and Government assistant professor, has been a go-to consultant for the World Health Organization and the City of Phoenix, Arizona.
