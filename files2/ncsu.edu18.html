The African American Cultural Center is currently practicing social distancing per the CDC and university guidelines, and thus is operating on a remote basis until further notice. As a result, all walk-in hours will be suspended; however, we will be available to schedule phone and virtual appointments, as needed. Please feel free to email aaculturalcenter@ncsu.edu for any questions or concerns.

We care about our Pack community. As such, we encourage you to follow CDC and university social distancing guidelines, stay safe and know that we are one community who prides itself on supporting one another and being united during challenging times.

Day of Giving

Giving to the African American Cultural Center's Enrichment Fund contributes to programming that empowers and encourages individual and collective agency through Black scholarship, culturally relevant leadership development and identity affirmation.

This Semester in the Cultural Center

Join us in Celebrating Black History Month

This year the African American Cultural Center and Multicultural Student Affairs recognizes the labor of Black, African, and African American people towards the liberation of all. We adopt the teachings of Black feminist, lesbian, scholar, poet Audre Lorde who believed in the naming and claiming of rest as a revolutionary action and that such a revolution would be our collective undoing and our liberation.
